# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India | Digital Ambassador Initiative

---

##  General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords for every account**
   Create passwords with at least 12 characters mixing uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager (like Bitwarden or Google Password Manager) so you never reuse passwords.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts**
   Turn on 2FA for email, social media, and banking apps. Even if someone steals your password, they cannot access your account without the second verification step (OTP/authenticator app).

3. **Never click links in unsolicited messages or emails**
   If you receive a text, WhatsApp message, or email with a link asking you to "verify your account," "claim a prize," or "avoid account suspension" — do NOT click it. Go directly to the official website instead.

4. **Verify the sender before sharing any personal information**
   Legitimate organisations (banks, colleges, government) will NEVER ask for your Aadhaar number, bank account details, OTP, or password via WhatsApp, SMS, or email.

5. **Keep your phone and apps updated**
   Software updates patch security vulnerabilities. Enable auto-updates for your OS and apps, especially your UPI and banking apps.

6. **Use secure, private Wi-Fi — avoid public hotspots for financial tasks**
   Never do internet banking, UPI transactions, or enter passwords on public/café/college Wi-Fi without a VPN. Attackers can intercept data on open networks.

---

##  Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

7. **Remember: RECEIVING money NEVER requires entering your UPI PIN**
   Your UPI PIN is ONLY needed when you SEND money. If anyone asks you to enter your PIN or scan a QR code to "receive" a payment — it is a SCAM. QR codes and collect requests deduct money, they do not add it.

8. **Always verify the UPI ID / phone number before approving any transaction**
   Before approving any collect request on PhonePe, GPay, or Paytm, check the requester's name and UPI ID carefully. When in doubt, call the person directly on a known number to confirm — never trust only the chat.

9. **Set a daily UPI transaction limit in your banking app**
   Most banks allow you to set a daily cap on UPI payments (e.g., ₹5,000/day). This limits your exposure if your phone or PIN is ever compromised.

10. **Register your SIM with your bank and enable SMS alerts for every transaction**
    Activate instant SMS/email alerts for all debits. The sooner you know about an unauthorised transaction, the faster you can block your account and report it.

---

##  Social Media & Privacy

11. **Audit your social media privacy settings every 3 months**
    Restrict your profile, phone number, and photos to "Friends Only" or "Contacts Only." Cybercriminals use public profiles to gather information for identity theft and targeted scams.

12. **Think before you post — digital footprints are permanent**
    Avoid posting your location in real-time, your college ID, travel plans, or financial details. Even "innocent" information can be combined to impersonate you.

---

##  Reporting Channels — Act Immediately if Victimised

| Resource | Details |
|---|---|
| **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — Report online financial fraud, cyberbullying, sextortion, identity theft, and more |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** — Call immediately after any financial fraud. The sooner you report, the higher the chance of freezing the fraudster's account |
| **Local Police (Cyber Cell)** | Visit your nearest police station's cyber cell with screenshots and transaction IDs |
| **Bank's Fraud Helpline** | Call your bank's 24×7 helpline immediately to block the account and dispute unauthorised transactions |

>  **Golden Rule:** In case of UPI fraud, call **1930** within the first hour. Banks can freeze the receiving account before the money is withdrawn.
