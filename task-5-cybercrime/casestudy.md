# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

**Cybercrime Type:** UPI / Online Payment Fraud  
**Prepared by:** Digital Ambassador | Task 5

---

## Overview

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India. It exploits the ease and trust people place in instant digital payments. Unlike hacking, this crime primarily manipulates victims psychologically — making them willingly hand over money or credentials.

---

## Realistic Scenario: "The Refund Trick"

**Victim:** Priya Sharma, 20, a second-year college student in Bhopal, selling her old laptop on OLX.

### Step-by-Step: How It Happened

1. **Contact:** A fraudster posing as an "Army officer" messaged Priya, expressing interest in buying her laptop. He appeared trustworthy.

2. **Urgency & Pressure:** He claimed he was posted in a remote area and needed to transfer money immediately via UPI before leaving.

3. **The "Collect Request" Trick:** The fraudster said, *"I'm sending ₹25,000 — just scan this QR code / accept this UPI request to receive the money."* He sent a **collect/debit request**, not a credit.

4. **Victim Enters PIN:** Priya, unfamiliar with how collect requests work, entered her UPI PIN to "accept" the payment — unknowingly **authorising a debit** of ₹18,000 from her account.

5. **Repeat & Disappear:** He called again claiming the first transfer "failed" and asked her to approve once more. After the second deduction, he blocked her number.

**Total Loss: ₹36,000**

---

## Who Is Usually Targeted?

- First-time or casual online sellers (OLX, Facebook Marketplace)
- Students and young adults new to UPI
- Senior citizens unfamiliar with digital transactions
- Small shopkeepers and gig workers

---

## Consequences

- **Financial:** Direct monetary loss, often unrecoverable
- **Psychological:** Anxiety, shame, loss of trust in digital payments
- **Legal complexity:** Victims often don't know how to report or believe nothing will come of it
- **Academic/life impact:** For students, such losses can cause significant financial hardship
