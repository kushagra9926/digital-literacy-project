# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

A responsible online presence checklist for College Students.

---

##  DO's

1. **Keep your profiles professional** — Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a bio that reflects your academic and career interests.

2. **Think before you post** — Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?" If not, don't post it.

3. **Use privacy settings** — Regularly review and update your privacy settings on all platforms to control who can see your content.

4. **Give credit when sharing** — Always credit the original creator when sharing someone else's work, articles, or images.

5. **Engage positively** — Support your peers' achievements, share useful academic content, and contribute constructively to discussions.

6. **Protect your personal information** — Avoid sharing your phone number, home address, Aadhaar/ID details, or financial information on any social platform.

7. **Report harmful content** — Use platform tools to report harassment, misinformation, or inappropriate content rather than engaging with it.

---

##  DON'Ts

1. **Don't post confidential or exam-related content** — Sharing question papers, internal marks, or college examination content online is a violation of academic integrity and can lead to disciplinary action.

2. **Don't engage in cyberbullying or trolling** — Mocking, harassing, or targeting peers, faculty, or public figures online — even anonymously — is harmful and can have legal consequences under the IT Act, 2000.

3. **Don't overshare your location in real time** — Posting live locations or check-ins can be a safety risk, especially for solo trips or late-night outings.

4. **Don't use social media to vent about college, faculty, or classmates** — Public complaints can damage relationships, your reputation, and may violate your institution's code of conduct.

5. **Don't click on suspicious links or "too good to be true" offers** — Fake scholarship offers, job scams, and phishing links are commonly circulated through social media DMs and posts.

6. **Don't impersonate others** — Creating fake profiles or pretending to be someone else is both unethical and illegal under Indian cyber law.

7. **Don't post copyrighted content without permission** — Uploading music, films, or articles you don't own rights to can lead to your account being suspended or worse.

---

## Quick Reminder

> Your digital footprint is permanent. What you post today can affect internship applications, college opportunities, and professional relationships years from now. Build your online presence with the same care you would bring to a face-to-face interaction.
